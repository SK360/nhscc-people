The Iron Nine
Nine drivers have competed in every single season from 2002 through 2026 — 25 consecutive years without missing one: Chris Carlisle, Dan Ennis, Dennis Witt, Doug Bober, Jay Gyger, Mark Sanner, Mike Ancas, Ted Weidner, and Walt Barie.
👑
Most Events Ever
Jay Gyger leads all-time with 206 career entries — appearing at roughly 94% of every event NHSCC has ever held. Second is Doug Bober and Chris Carlisle, each with 182.
🚗
Most Loyal Car
Doug Bober has entered his 1988 Pontiac Fiero GT an astonishing 174 times since 2002 — the most-entered specific driver/car combination in the entire dataset.
🚪
Half Never Come Back
Of the 3,200+ unique drivers ever to enter an NHSCC event, 50.4% appeared exactly once. Only about 1 in 5 drivers has come back more than 5 times.
❤️
The Weaver Pair
Ralph Weaver (162 entries) and Jenny Weaver (137 entries) have competed side by side for 24 consecutive years, from 2002 through 2025.
📅
Greatest Comeback
Mitch Stivason ran in 2003, then vanished from the results for 20 years — returning to competition in 2023. The longest gap between first and last appearance in the database.
🏎️
Miata Is Always the Answer
The Mazda Miata has 759 entries across 25 different car classes — the most-entered model and the most class-versatile car in NHSCC history. It has been entered continuously since 2002.
🧽
The Spongebob Miata
A 1990/91 Mazda Miata nicknamed "Spongebob" was a shared club car campaigned in XP class from 2013–2019. At least 11 different drivers took a turn behind the wheel.
⏱️
Courses Got Much Faster
Average raw times have dropped from roughly 45 seconds in 2002–2003 to about 31 seconds in recent years — a 31% reduction reflecting shorter, tighter course designs over two decades.
🎯
Class Tourists
Chris Carlisle has competed in 26 different car classes; Jay Gyger in 25. Between them they've sampled nearly every class NHSCC has ever offered.
📈
Busiest Event & Season
The single busiest event was May 26, 2013 with 153 entries. The biggest full season was 2025, which saw 1,020 total entries across 13 events.
⚡
EVs Are Coming
Electric vehicles barely registered before 2020, but the EVX class has grown steadily — logging 163 entries in 2025–2026 alone. The revolution is already in the paddock.

🏁 FTD (Fastest Time of Day) = the single lowest raw time recorded at each event. Course designs change every event, so times are not comparable across events — only win counts matter here.
